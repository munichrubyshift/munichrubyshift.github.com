Meet, learn & have fun

Welcome! We are are happy you found us.
We are the Munich Ruby user group and we gladly invite you to our next meetup.
Meet regularily once a month to learn new and interesting stuff about Ruby, to foster our community and to have fun together.


Our objectives

Speakers, talks & discussions

We meet every second Wednesday of the month at 19:00 CET at varying locations.
We will have up to 4 presentations between 10-20 min each and discussions afterwards.
Do you have an idea for an interesting topic concerning Ruby or something related? Please submit a talk!


The next meetup

Nerds & Newbies

So you are new and interested in Ruby? Yet afraid to join because all of that is detailed for you just right now? Don't worry! We have beginners talks.
And you? You are a hyper-nerd and afraid because you could be bored. Don't worry we have a few of your kind as well. Rest assured you will find someone to have a meaning full discussion.

Henning Koch
Solving bizarre authorization requirements
When it comes to user permissions ("who is allowed to do what?")
there is no limit to the human imagination. As a development shop
that works for a large number of clients, we've seen many
strange authorization requirements in the wild. Such requirements
tend to be full of case switching and exceptions and can quickly infect
other application concerns if you don't pay attention.
This talk proposes a number of simple design guidelines to deal with
the most byzantine authorization requirements in a concise and structured way.
These guidelines have been serving us well in many production projects over the years.
Code examples will be in Ruby on Rails,
but the techniques demonstrated are applicable to other technologies as well.
