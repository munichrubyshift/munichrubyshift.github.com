Christoph Engelhardt on Twilio

I'm 28 years old, married, work as an officer, study applied computer technology. started programming at age 14 (mainly C#, Java, PHP and C). Started Ruby in February.

I will talk about twilio (http://twilio.com). Twilio brings voice and messaging to your web and mobile applications.

I plan to cover: (presentation and free style)

  • buying a phone number
  • creating and setting up the twilio application (model on their part - not the coding)
  • pricing
  • restrictions and caveats

The Demo App covers: (all via API)

  • verifying a phone number you own
  • send / receive text messages
  • call a phone
  • read messages via speech engine
  • playback mp3
  • gather key press
  • make a conference call
  • record a call
  • transcribe a recording

Henning Koch on Solving bizarre authorization requirements

When it comes to user permissions ("who is allowed to do what?") there is no limit to the human imagination. As a development shop that works for a large number of clients, we've seen many strange authorization requirements in the wild. Such requirements tend to be full of case switching and exceptions and can quickly infect other application concerns if you don't pay attention.

This talk proposes a number of simple design guidelines to deal with the most byzantine authorization requirements in a concise and structured way. These guidelines have been serving us well in many production projects over the years.

Code examples will be in Ruby on Rails, but the techniques demonstrated are applicable to other technologies as well.
